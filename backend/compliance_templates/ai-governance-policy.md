## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes {{CITY_NAME}}'s framework for the responsible acquisition, deployment, use, and oversight of artificial intelligence (AI) systems in municipal operations. It ensures that AI tools serve the public interest, maintain human authority over government decisions, protect resident data, and uphold principles of transparency, accountability, and fairness.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All AI systems acquired, developed, or deployed by {{CITY_NAME}} departments.
- All municipal employees, contractors, and vendors who develop, deploy, operate, or interact with AI systems on behalf of {{CITY_NAME}}.
- Both internally developed and externally procured AI tools.
- AI systems used in any capacity, including pilot programs, proof-of-concept deployments, and production use.

**Exclusions:** Consumer-grade tools used incidentally by employees (e.g., smartphone autocorrect, standard email spam filters) are excluded unless they process municipal data or influence government decisions.

## 3. Governing Principles

### 3.1 Human Authority

AI systems shall augment, not replace, human judgment in government operations. No AI system shall make final decisions on matters that affect residents' rights, access to services, or legal obligations without meaningful human review and approval. Human operators retain the authority and obligation to override, modify, or reject AI outputs at all times.

### 3.2 Transparency

{{CITY_NAME}} shall be open about its use of AI:

- Public disclosures shall describe each AI system's purpose, capabilities, and limitations.
- When AI contributes to decisions affecting individuals, those individuals shall be informed.
- AI systems used by {{CITY_NAME}} shall be explainable — staff must be able to describe, in plain language, how the system reaches its outputs.
- {{CITY_NAME}} shall prefer open-source AI solutions where feasible to enable public inspection and independent audit.

### 3.3 Data Sovereignty

{{CITY_NAME}} shall maintain control over its data:

- Municipal data shall not be transmitted to external cloud services, third-party servers, or vendor systems without explicit authorization and a data processing agreement.
- AI systems that process sensitive municipal data shall run on locally owned and operated infrastructure unless a specific exception is granted.
- No municipal data shall be used by vendors to train AI models without explicit written authorization.
- {{CITY_NAME}} retains ownership of all data, outputs, and derivatives produced by AI systems.

### 3.4 Accountability

Clear lines of responsibility shall exist for every AI system:

- Each AI system shall have a designated owner (department head or designee) responsible for its proper use and oversight.
- Decisions informed by AI remain the responsibility of the human decision-maker, not the AI system.
- {{CITY_NAME}} shall maintain audit trails of AI-informed decisions sufficient to reconstruct the basis for any decision.
- Vendors providing AI systems shall be contractually required to support {{CITY_NAME}}'s accountability obligations.

### 3.5 Fairness and Non-Discrimination

AI systems shall not introduce or amplify bias:

- Before deployment, AI systems that influence decisions affecting residents shall be assessed for potential disparate impact on protected classes.
- {{CITY_NAME}} shall not deploy AI systems that use race, ethnicity, gender, religion, national origin, disability status, or other protected characteristics as input features unless required by law and approved by the city attorney.
- Ongoing monitoring shall track AI outputs for patterns that may indicate bias.
- Residents shall have the right to request human review of any AI-influenced decision.

## 4. AI System Approval Process

### 4.1 Inventory

{{CITY_NAME}} shall maintain a current inventory of all AI systems in use, including:

- System name and vendor (if applicable)
- Department owner and point of contact
- Purpose and capabilities
- Data sources and data types processed
- Risk classification
- Date of deployment and most recent review

### 4.2 Pre-Deployment Review

Before deploying any new AI system or significantly modifying an existing one, the requesting department shall complete the following:

1. **Use case documentation:** Describe the problem the AI system addresses, the expected benefits, and the alternatives considered.
2. **Risk assessment:** Classify the system's risk level based on the nature of decisions it informs and the populations affected.
3. **Data impact analysis:** Document what data the system accesses, how it is processed, where it is stored, and what data protection measures are in place.
4. **Bias assessment:** For systems that influence decisions affecting residents, evaluate potential for disparate impact.
5. **Vendor review:** For procured systems, review vendor data practices, security certifications, and contractual obligations.
6. **Legal review:** City attorney review for compliance with applicable federal, state, and local law.

### 4.3 Approval Authority

| Risk Level | Description | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Internal productivity tools that do not process sensitive data or influence decisions affecting residents | Department head |
| Medium | Systems that process sensitive data or provide advisory input to staff decisions | Department head + IT director + city manager review |
| High | Systems that are a substantial factor in decisions affecting residents' rights, services, or obligations | City manager + city attorney + governing body notification |

### 4.4 Pilot Programs

Pilot deployments of AI systems are encouraged to evaluate effectiveness before full deployment, subject to:

- Time-limited scope (maximum 6 months without renewal).
- Same data protection requirements as production deployments.
- Defined success criteria and evaluation plan.
- Department head approval at minimum.

## 5. Ongoing Compliance Requirements

### 5.1 Periodic Review

- Each AI system shall be reviewed **annually** by its department owner to confirm continued compliance with this policy.
- Reviews shall assess: accuracy and performance, staff training currency, data governance, bias monitoring results, and continued alignment with the approved use case.
- Review findings shall be reported to the IT director.

### 5.2 Training

- All staff who use or oversee AI systems shall complete initial training before using the system.
- Training shall cover: system capabilities and limitations, human oversight responsibilities, data handling requirements, and how to report concerns.
- Annual refresher training is required.

### 5.3 Incident Response

When an AI system produces an erroneous or harmful output that affects a resident or municipal operation:

1. The department owner shall be notified immediately.
2. The AI system's use may be suspended pending investigation at the discretion of the department owner or IT director.
3. An incident report shall be completed within 30 days documenting: what occurred, root cause, impact, and corrective actions.
4. Material incidents shall be reported to the city manager.

### 5.4 Vendor Obligations

Contracts with AI vendors shall include:

- Prohibition on using {{CITY_NAME}} data to train vendor models without explicit authorization.
- Data residency and security requirements.
- Right to audit vendor AI systems and data practices.
- Incident notification requirements.
- Clear intellectual property ownership terms.

### 5.5 Public Reporting

{{CITY_NAME}} shall publish an annual summary of AI systems in use, including their purposes and any material changes, to maintain public trust and transparency.

## 7. Definitions

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** A machine-based system that generates outputs such as predictions, recommendations, classifications, or decisions for a given set of human-defined objectives.
- **AI System Owner:** The department head or designee responsible for the deployment, use, and oversight of a specific AI system.
- **Consequential Decision:** A decision that materially affects a resident's rights, access to government services, or legal obligations.
- **Human-in-the-Loop:** A system design in which a human reviews and approves AI outputs before they take effect.
